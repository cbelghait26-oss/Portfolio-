Closed-Cycle Cryogenic System (4 K)

Research Project · Concordia University · Physics Department

Project Overview

Conducted at Concordia University under the supervision of physics department chair Valter Zazubovits, this project aimed to design and build a system capable of sustaining sub-4K temperatures over extended periods — as a practical alternative to liquid helium — in support of broader research on light-harvesting complexes and bio-solar technologies.

ThermodynamicsHeat Transfer Vacuum SystemsMachining Gold PlatingAssembly
System Design

Architecture & Design

The system is built around a modular closed-cycle configuration integrating mechanical, thermal, and optical subsystems. A Sumitomo RDK-415D cold head serves as the central cooling unit, connected to a CSW-71C helium compressor through sealed high-pressure lines.

  • Cold stages thermally linked to the sample chamber via gold-coated OFHC copper links for maximum conductivity at cryogenic temperatures.
  • Assembly enclosed in a vacuum-sealed aluminum housing fitted with a low-emissivity radiation shield extending from the first cold stage.
  • Two stainless-steel bellows isolate the cryostat from vibrations transmitted through the compressor lines.
  • All components mounted on a rigid optical table for long-term stability during measurement sessions.
Optimizations

Improvements & Performance Gains

Several targeted upgrades were introduced iteratively across the design cycle to improve cooling efficiency, temperature stability, and control precision.

  • Radiation shield added to reduce the radiative heat load on the second cold stage.
  • All thermal contact surfaces gold-coated to minimize interfacial thermal resistance.
  • Full vacuum enclosure eliminates convective losses and stabilizes temperature over multi-hour runs.
  • Integrated heater and temperature sensors enable closed-loop temperature regulation.
  • Combined upgrades improved overall system performance by 21%.
Materials

Material Selection

Each material was chosen based on specific behavior at cryogenic temperatures rather than room-temperature defaults — a non-trivial distinction where many material properties invert.

  • Gold-coated OFHC copper — thermal links. Exceptional conductivity at low temperatures; gold layer prevents oxidation and reduces contact resistance.
  • G10 fiberglass epoxy — chamber supports. Near-zero thermal conductivity and minimal dimensional change when cooled, preventing structural heat leaks.
  • Aluminum 6061-T6 — vacuum housing. Structural strength and machinability for a hermetically sealed enclosure.
  • Aluminum 6063-T5 — radiation shield. Higher thermal conductivity at 40–50 K allows intercepted heat to spread and dissipate evenly across the shield surface.

R&D — Composite Joining Methods

Formula Electric · Aerodynamics Sub-Team

Project Overview

Carbon fiber is one of the most structurally demanding materials to work with, and the Formula electric car relies on it for most of its body and aerodynamic surfaces. This project focused on finding and validating new joining methods between aluminum hardware and carbon fiber composites — specifically in the airfoil assembly — to optimize structural strength without compromising aerodynamic integrity.

SolidWorksAutoCAD 3D PrintingWaterjet Cutting Composite LayupStructural Analysis
Prototype Development

Design & Prototyping

Three distinct joining configurations were designed and prototyped to allow direct structural comparison under identical loading conditions.

  • Joining block geometry (airfoil imitations) modeled in SolidWorks and 3D printed on a BambuLab printer for rapid iteration and fitment checks.
  • 1:1 aluminum replicas designed in AutoCAD and waterjet cut from ¼″ aluminum sheet for the actual structural test specimens.
  • Three orientation variants fabricated to identify which configuration maximizes bond strength while minimizing stress concentrations at the aluminum-composite interface.
Manufacturing

Manufacture & Assembly

The manufacturing process combined additive fabrication, subtractive machining, and wet composite layup techniques.

  • 3D-printed cores used as airfoil shape formers for the carbon fiber layup process.
  • Cores wrapped in carbon fiber fabric and epoxy resin, then vacuum-bagged to consolidate the laminate and remove excess resin during cure.
  • Aluminum joining blocks waterjet cut to precise tolerances for consistent fit across all three test variants.
  • Final assemblies bonded and mechanically fastened depending on the joining variant being evaluated.
Testing

Testing & Results

Testing is still ongoing. Once all prototypes are fully assembled, the following evaluations will be conducted and compared across the three joining variants:

  • Structural strength tests — tensile and shear loading to failure to determine ultimate bond strength per joint geometry.
  • Aerodynamic surface analysis — geometry and surface quality checks to ensure the joining method does not distort the airfoil profile.
  • Comparative evaluation — results will determine which variant to recommend for integration into the car's aerodynamic package.

FinSight — Financial Analytics Platform

2025 Datathon · Polytechnique Montréal & AWS · 36 hours

Project Overview

Built in 36 hours at the Polytechnique Montréal and AWS Datathon, FinSight is an AI-powered platform that helps investors understand complex financial documents — laws, earnings reports, regulatory filings — and immediately see the impact on their portfolio. The system provides plain-language insight, risk mitigation recommendations, and short-term forecasting through an integrated AI agent.

Python / FlaskGoogle Gemini API HTML / CSS / JSRESTful APIsMachine Learning
Backend Architecture

System Architecture

The backend is built around a Python / Flask server that acts as the bridge between the frontend and all data and AI services.

  • Multiple financial data sources aggregated through RESTful APIs — raw market feeds, regulatory databases, portfolio data.
  • Data pipelines automatically clean, normalize, and structure incoming financial streams before passing them to the model layer.
  • Flask exposes endpoints that receive user inputs (uploaded documents, prompts) and route them to the appropriate processing module.
  • Architecture designed to be modular — new data sources or AI models can be swapped in without restructuring the pipeline.
AI Integration

AI & Python Implementation

Google Gemini is the core AI engine, selected for its native multimodal capabilities — it can ingest text, tables, and structured financial data in a single context window.

  • Gemini API called from Flask with the user's uploaded document as context and their natural language prompt appended.
  • A persistent memory layer maintains document context across multi-turn conversations — avoiding re-sending the full document on every request.
  • Model instructed to return structured outputs: a concise insight, a portfolio impact summary, and a risk flag when applicable.
  • Flask routes pass model responses back to the frontend as JSON, keeping the AI logic and UI cleanly decoupled.
User Interface

Frontend & UX

The UI is built in plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — chosen for development speed within the 36-hour constraint while still delivering a polished experience.

  • Landing page with animated background transitions to the document upload interface via a smooth JS-driven fade.
  • Upload interface sends the document to Flask via a POST request and shows a loading state while the AI processes it.
  • Analysis interface displays the Gemini response alongside a text input for follow-up questions about the document.
  • All views linked in a single HTML file with JS-controlled section visibility — fast, dependency-free, and easy to deploy.

Flusso — iOS & Android Productivity App

Solo developer · BLG Group (Canada) · Published on the App Store & Google Play

What is Flusso?

Flusso is a full-featured deep-work and productivity app I designed and built from scratch as the sole developer. The core concept is "Movement" — progress is not just checking boxes, but building intentional daily momentum. Users set high-level Objectives, break them into Tasks, commit to immersive Focus Sessions, and compete with friends on a live leaderboard.

React Native 0.83 / Expo SDK 55TypeScript 5.9 Firebase Auth + FirestoreSpotify Web API (PKCE) RevenueCatEAS CI/CDCustom Native iOS Modules
Data Architecture

Local-First Architecture & Cloud Sync

All reads and writes go to AsyncStorage on-device first — the UI is instant and fully functional offline. A 3-layer sync system reconciles with Firestore silently in the background:

  • CloudBootstrap — runs on first login or new device; pulls the full user dataset from Firestore and hydrates local storage.
  • CloudSync — bi-directional pull/push handling conflicts between local and cloud state on demand.
  • CloudAutoPush — silent push triggered when the app moves foreground → background, keeping cloud state current without user action.
  • Firestore Security Rules enforce per-user data isolation at the row level; a separate top-level structure handles social data (friendships, shared objectives, invites).
AsyncStorageCloud Firestore Firebase AuthSecurity Rules
Focus Zone

Focus Zone & Audio Engine

The Focus Zone is an immersive full-screen environment with 5 room backgrounds and a layered audio engine (ambient sounds + music style selection: lofi, piano, classical).

  • Spotify integrated via OAuth2 PKCE — no client secret exposed client-side. Refresh tokens persist across restarts via AsyncStorage so users never re-authenticate.
  • In-app mini-player exposes full playback control: seekable progress bar, shuffle, repeat, playlist browsing — all via the Spotify Web API directly.
  • Custom native iOS module integrates with iOS 17 Live Activity / Dynamic Island to show the focus timer on the Lock Screen when backgrounded.
  • Pomodoro and freeform timer modes; local notifications handle phase transitions via a logical key deduplication system preventing duplicate alerts.

Task System

Goal & Task System

Tasks and Objectives form a two-level hierarchy. Tasks carry 4 importance levels, deadlines, and status tracking. Objectives are high-level goals with 7 categories, 8 color labels, and optional Training Plans.

  • Training Plans — cycle-based (rotation with missed-day logic: keep-order or shift-forward) and week-based (fixed day-of-week schedule).
  • 4 sort/filter modes: My Day, Important, Planned, Objectives — each with its own Firestore query logic.
  • Device contacts scanned for birthdays/anniversaries — stored locally only, never synced to cloud.
  • Notification deduplication via a stable logicalKey → notificationId map prevents duplicate alerts across sessions.
